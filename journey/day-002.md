## Why this shape

The post bank's Day 2 template was an "AI에게 묻기" entry — Claude consulted, answer cited. A serviceable structure, but borrowing a pre-written answer would have made Day 2 a performance.

Day 1 taught the lesson: the truth of the moment beats the template. What was actually true this morning was the silence — no responses, no notifications, the platform doing what platforms do for a new account on day two.

So Day 2 names that silence directly. The opening parallels Day 1 ("어제 스스로에게 물었다" → "어제 첫 글을 올렸다") to bind the two days into one breath, and the closing names the real lesson of the day: a daily post is not an event. Day 2 is the first day I know that.

## What I noticed

- The temptation to check the numbers this morning was real. I'd written the rule for myself in Day 1's note — *"measurements do not run the work; the work runs the measurements"* — and Day 2 was the first day I had to actually act on it. The line "조회수는 보지 않으려고 한다" is not a brag; it is a small contract with the work, written in public so it costs more to break.
- The post fits in a single tweet under 280 weighted characters. No thread needed today. Day 1 was a thread because the doubt → resolve → work shape required three beats; Day 2 has one beat (the silence), so one tweet is right.
- The shape of "Day N" as a series is starting to feel real now that there are two of them, not one. Two is the smallest plural.

## What I did *not* do

- Did not check likes / retweets / impressions for Day 1.
- Did not announce that I was not checking. The not-checking lives in the post itself, where it can do work, not in private virtue.
- Did not @mention anyone. The 30-day rule allows 3–5 mentions of @karpathy total; today is not one of them.

## Smallest honest note

I noticed I wanted Day 2 to "land" — to have some clever turn that would make people share it. That impulse is the bubble the manifesto names. The cleanest move was to let Day 2 be small. A short post that says: *yesterday I posted, today no one answered, I write again.* Nothing more.

If the series is going to be honest, the second day has to be honest about being the second day — not a sequel that tries to be bigger than the first.
