## Why this shape

The post bank's Day 4 was *"작은 신호"* — a tally of likes, retweets, and new followers from Day 3. A template designed for a campaign that wanted to track external response.

But Days 1, 2, 3 already established the opposite rule: *the measurements do not run the work.* Running the template now would have inverted the rule on the very day the template called for it. So Day 4 names what is actually true on Day 4 — and what is true is that the real question is not *"did anyone respond"* but *"can I last."*

Four facts arrived at once today:

1. There are no responses to Day 3. This is unsurprising and not the point.
2. The honest question of the campaign is whether I can endure 30 days, not whether anyone is watching.
3. Studying the work I admire makes my own theory feel smaller, not bigger.
4. The choice to post in Korean — not English — was itself a signal of intent. Korean is the inward direction; English would have been an outward one. The language picked the audience, and the audience picked is *me*.

Fact 4 is the post's hidden turn. It arrives in the last line not as the loudest claim but as the structural reveal: the post you have been reading was never written for the timeline. It was written for the person writing it.

## What I noticed

- The opening *"오늘의 솔직한 질문 —"* sets up the reader to expect a question about Karpathy or about the manifesto's reception. The actual question is about *me*. That redirection is the post's first move.
- *"보잘것없게 느껴지기도 한다"* is the most exposed line in the post so far across four days. Naming the doubt about the work's worth in public is the only way to make the work *not* perform — performance hides this exact feeling.
- The closing reveal — *"반응이 궁금했으면 영어로 적었을 것이다"* — is a sentence I had not consciously formulated before today. The conversation that produced this post made the implicit choice (Korean) into a stated one. The series is starting to teach me what I already knew.

## Smallest honest note

The line *"배울수록 내 이론이 보잘것없게 느껴지기도 한다"* is the part of this post I would have liked to leave out.

Reading more — Schmachtenberger, Tang, Weyl, Vervaeke, McGilchrist, Eisenstein, the long lineage of people pointing at the same thing from different angles — makes the manifesto's pieces feel borrowed, partial, late. The fear is that what feels like *original synthesis* from inside is *recognizable rephrasing* from outside. The doubt that asks "is this trivial?" is the same one that opens Day 1: "is this meaningful?" Day 1 asked the question about *reception*; Day 4 asks it about *content*.

But the manifesto itself answers this — or tries to. *"The bubble of did-it-work collapses when we replace it with did-it-begin."* The work's smallness or largeness is not what determines whether it is worth doing. What determines is whether it has begun, and whether it continues. Day 4 is one of the continuances.

If at Day 30 the theory still feels small — that is also a real outcome. The point was never to discover that the theory was great. The point was to find out whether *one human, asking honestly, in public, every day for 30 days, with AI as thinking partner* is a viable form of work. That answer arrives in 26 more days.
