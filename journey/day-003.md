## Why this shape

The post bank's Day 3 was supposed to be "매니페스토 발췌 1" — a quote from the manifesto. A third pre-written excerpt would have been the easy move.

But this weekend a friend said something that the manifesto exists to answer, and pretending that didn't happen — in order to post a clean quote on schedule — would have inverted the whole project. So Day 3 takes the actual moment.

The friend's words contain three layers:

1. *사차원* — a soft labeling. Not "you're wrong," but "you're not like us." Distance via classification.
2. *먹고살 생각부터* — an ordering claim. Survival first, then meaning. Anything else is luxury or evasion.
3. *진심* — and the friend was sincere. Worried, not malicious. Which is heavier than malice.

I could not continue the conversation in the moment. The honest thing was to say so on the page, and to mark the *next* move (a different way of speaking to the friend) as the open task.

The line that the post turns on — *"부자라서가 아니라 부자라 생각하기로 했기 때문에"* — is not a slogan. It is the **sovereign filter** principle applied to self-evaluation: I do not let the social definition of "rich" decide whether my life is satisfied. That definition stays on the social side; the satisfaction lives on mine.

This is the first day in the 30 that a manifesto principle came out of a lived moment rather than from re-reading the document.

---

## What I noticed

- The friend's question was correct on its own terms. The post does not pretend otherwise. Defending the work by dismissing the question would have been the bubble — performance over honesty. Naming the question as correct *and* staying on this side of it is the move the manifesto says is possible.
- The phrasing *"맞는 말이다"* arrives in line four. If it had arrived later — or never — the post would read as an argument. Putting the agreement up front lets what comes after read as a *position*, not a *defense*.
- Closing with *"이 한 줄을 친구에게 다른 방식으로 풀어보려 한다"* leaves the post open. The work continues offline, in a real conversation, with a real person. The 30-day series is not the whole project — it is the surface of the project.

## Smallest honest note

I did not expect the manifesto's central move to arrive through a friend's worry. I expected it to come from sustained reflection, alone, at a screen. Instead it came in a sentence I spoke without planning to: *부자가 아니라, 부자라 생각하기로 했다.*

If the 30 days produce nothing else but lived sentences like this one, the campaign will have done its work. Karpathy is the destination on the map; sentences like this are the road.

Day 3 came on the 27th, not the 25th. The friend's words were spoken mid-trip, on a vacation we took together over those two days. The vacation was the room the question was asked in, and the room I could not answer in. The campaign is younger than the friendship, and stays younger.

> Day 3은 25일이 아니라 27일에 도착했다. 친구의 그 말은, 우리가 함께 떠났던 이틀의 여행 중 어느 자리에서 나왔다. 휴가가 그 질문이 던져진 방이었고, 내가 답하지 못한 방이었다. 캠페인은 우정보다 어린 것이고, 계속 어린 것이다.
