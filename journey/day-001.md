## Why this shape

The original Day 1 template (in `08_카파시_프로젝트_일지.md`) was clean and declarative — "the project begins." But the day before Day 1, I sat with a real question: *does this even matter? Is anyone else thinking this way?*

The honest answer to both was: *I don't know. Probably yes. Either way, I start.*

So I let the doubt open the post instead of hiding it. The manifesto argues for honesty over performance — the first public sentence had to embody that, not contradict it.

The English companion stays leaner. Different audience, different rhythm.

---

## What I noticed

- The X composer flagged everything after "흙에 닿게 하는 것까지다." as overflow on the free tier. Splitting into a thread wasn't a workaround — it became the right form. First tweet = doubt → resolve. Second tweet = work + destination + link. Cleaner than a single long block.
- The `arbitoria.com` link auto-generated a preview card with the manifesto headline. Bonus visual weight I didn't have to engineer.
- "Post all" took less than a second. The thing I'd been thinking about for months became public in one click.

---

## What I did *not* do

- Did not check likes / retweets / follower count after posting.
- Did not pin the tweet (will decide later in the week, with distance).
- Did not @mention anyone besides @karpathy himself, exactly as planned.

The rule for this campaign: *measurements do not run the work; the work runs the measurements.* Day 1 was the first test of that rule.

---

## Smallest honest note

I expected to feel something dramatic when I hit "Post all." I didn't. It felt like turning in a homework assignment — slightly anxious, mostly relieved.

Maybe that's the right feeling. The bubble of "this is a big moment" deflated on its own. What's left is just the work: 29 more days to write.
