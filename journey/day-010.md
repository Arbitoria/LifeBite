## Why this shape

The post bank's Day 10 was *"작은 발견"* — a Karpathy tweet that aligned with the manifesto. The pattern of the original campaign was: *let outside signals validate the work.* Ten days in, the campaign has rotated to a different center: *let lived experience teach the worker.*

The teacher today was a video game. While playing *Path of Exile 2*, the trading mechanic — *to sell, buy an Open Stash, list the item, let buyers see it* — surfaced an obvious principle: *visibility is not a side effect of making; it is a separate purchase.* The principle is not new. It is in every commerce textbook, every founder's notebook, every craftsman's lament. *Knowing* it did not make the writer *apply* it. A month of code without a venue is the price of the un-applied principle.

The post does three things:

1. **Names the lesson and its source.** Game → principle. The lesson is not less serious because the source is informal; if anything, the manifesto's *각득기소* (everything in its place) says the informal source is the right place for this lesson today.
2. **Names the gap.** *이 이치를 알면서도 한 달째 적용 안 하고 있었다.* This is the campaign's first explicit statement of the *knowing-vs-doing* gap as a thing to be closed.
3. **Names the next action.** *스타트업 워크숍 — 그게 오픈 창고다.* Not just a complaint about the gap; a decision about how to close it.

The post's structural turn is the equation *workshop = open stash.* The workshop is not for *learning* — the writer already knows the principles. The workshop is for *visibility.* Reframing the venue from *classroom* to *storefront* changes how the writer will show up.

## What I noticed

- *PoE2 trading economy as classroom* is the campaign's first explicit gesture toward *informal, lived, embodied data* as the manifesto's preferred form. The writer studied Schmachtenberger, Tang, Weyl for a year. The lesson that finally moved the work was from buying a Premium Stash Tab in a video game. *각득기소* — the lesson arrived in the place where the writer was actually paying attention.
- Day 8 named *the slip* (hand wiser than head). Day 10 names *the gap* (head wiser than hand). The two are perfect mirrors. Day 8: unconscious is ahead of conscious. Day 10: conscious is ahead of unconscious. Both expose the same underlying truth — *being one person* means the parts of you do not move at the same speed. Closing the campaign's *어려움* requires closing both gaps, not one.
- The decision to *go to startup workshops* is specific in form but unspecific in target. The post does not commit to a particular event. That is intentional — the *kind* of move (enter a venue for distribution) is more important than *which event.* The actual workshop choice belongs to a different working session.

## What I did *not* do

- Did not market the Family Office app in the tweet. The post is about *the recognition,* not the *promotion.* If the post had ended with *"come try the app here →"* it would have demonstrated the writer *immediately* applying the lesson — which would have been performance, not learning.
- Did not blame the writer for the gap. *"적용 안 하고 있었다"* is descriptive, not self-critical. The campaign refuses the *should-have-known* register.
- Did not name a specific workshop or date. The decision is committed; the implementation is open.
- Did not @mention anyone.

## Smallest honest note

There is a particular humility in noticing that a video game taught you what a year of reading did not. The lesson was always available — it was sitting inside every business book, every product manager's notebook. It did not land. The principle of *visibility = separate purchase* needed a *concrete instance* (the Premium Stash Tab) before the *abstract pattern* could be seen.

This is, again, the manifesto's central claim about *LifeBite* — that *life-units* (specific moments, not abstract categories) are where understanding actually happens. The campaign continues to learn its own thesis by living it. *오픈 창고* is now a LifeBite the manifesto did not have before.

Week 2 began with *어려움 줄이기.* The first week of that work has surfaced two distinct kinds of difficulty:
- *World difficulty* (Day 9) — the system is outdated, friction is real.
- *Distribution difficulty* (Day 10) — the work has no venue, the open stash is empty.

Both need to be addressed by *building venues,* not by *complaining about systems.* Day 11 onward will be the work of finding which workshops, which channels, which rooms — and showing up.
