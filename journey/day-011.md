## Why this shape

The post bank's Day 11 was *"AI에게 새로운 방법"* — yet another consultation framing. By Day 11 the campaign has already left consultation behind. The work has become a record of *what actually happens in a single human being on a single day,* and on this day what happened was nothing.

Day 10 ended with a clear strategic decision: *오픈 창고가 필요하다 — 스타트업 워크숍부터 간다.* Day 11 admits the decision did not translate to action. A few minutes of search, signup, DM — *the work that would have been the start* — sat undone. Not because it was hard. Because *ease itself does not produce the start.*

The post does three things:

1. **Names the ease.** *쉬운 일이다 / 몇 분 안 걸린다.* The post refuses the lie that the obstacle is difficulty. The obstacle is not difficulty.
2. **Names the not-doing in the passive voice.** *"안 하게 된다."* The grammar matters. Not *"I don't do it"* (I am the agent of not-doing) but *"it ends up not being done"* (something flows around me that ends in not-doing). The passive is more accurate to the lived experience. Resisting the temptation to write the active form is part of the post's honesty.
3. **Names the gap between the writer of yesterday and the writer of today.** *어제 적은 사람* and *오늘 못 사고 있는 사람* are the same person, twenty hours apart, and the second one cannot complete what the first one named. The campaign now has a public record of a *promise not kept by oneself.*

The closing — *쉬움은 시작이 아니다* — is the single line of insight the post permits itself. It says: *do not believe that low cost is the same as low threshold.* The cost of the workshop signup is minutes. The *threshold* is something else. The campaign now has an open question about what *threshold* is, and that question will travel.

## What I noticed

- The three days Day 8 (*손이 머리보다 정확*), Day 10 (*머리는 알았는데 손이 안 갔다*), Day 11 (*결정했는데 손이 안 간다*) form a complete map of the *knowing-doing gap.* Day 8: the hand was ahead. Day 10: the head was ahead but the hand had not moved. Day 11: even after the head named the move explicitly, the hand still did not move. The map says: *being one person does not mean the parts of you cooperate.* The campaign's second half will need to address how cooperation happens — *systems, structures, rituals,* not *resolutions.*
- *쉬움이 시작을 만들지 않는다* is the inverse of every productivity manual the writer has read. The manuals say *if it is small, you will do it.* The manuals are wrong about the specific case of *the small thing that matters.* Small + meaningful = harder to start, not easier. The cost is low and the *stakes are felt* — and stakes that are felt produce a threshold the cost does not predict.
- Day 11 is the first post that contains *no resolution.* Days 1-10 all closed with either a continuance (*"그래도 한 편 더"*), a discovery (*"손가락이 머리보다 먼저 알았다"*), or a decision (*"스타트업 워크숍부터 간다"*). Day 11 closes with a *diagnostic* (*"쉬움은 시작이 아니다"*) but no *next action.* This is intentional. Posting a next action today would have papered over the gap that was the day's actual content.

## What I did *not* do

- Did not promise to do the un-done thing tomorrow. Promising would have been the bubble — using the post to extract a commitment that the post is supposed to be a record of *not yet making.*
- Did not name the specific un-done action. The post stays at the level of *the form of the gap.* Specifying the action (e.g., *"디캠프 신청"*) would have foregrounded a particular venue when the day's real subject is *the threshold itself.*
- Did not blame the writer. *"안 하게 된다"* is reported, not condemned.
- Did not @mention anyone.

## Smallest honest note

The hardest sentence to write today was *"안 하게 된다."* Not because it was painful — because it sounds like complaint. The line had to be honest enough to *be a record* and restrained enough to *not be a confession.* The passive voice ended up doing both: it removes the self from the center of blame while leaving the not-doing in full view.

There is a quiet failure mode where the daily campaign becomes *a manifesto about the manifesto* — every day generating a new principle, every day extracting a new line of wisdom. Day 11 refuses that. The day produced nothing visible; the post is a record of that nothing. If the next nineteen days never recover from the not-doing, that too will be a true campaign. If the next nineteen days find a way through, *that route will have been earned* by today's honest stop.

The manifesto's *각득기소* says everything belongs in its right place. *Not-doing* belongs in its right place. Today the right place is *here, on the page, with the cost named but the act not yet completed.* Tomorrow will arrive in nineteen hours and find out what *its* right place is.
