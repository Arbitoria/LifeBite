# For Researchers

The manifesto introduces several concepts that are currently at
vocabulary level. They need theoretical development.

## Concepts in Need of Refinement

**LifeBite**
What is the formal definition? Is it an information-theoretic unit?
A time unit? A meaning unit? Is there a measurable "richness" or
"resolution" of a LifeBite? How does it relate to existing concepts
in phenomenology (qualia), philosophy of mind (mental states), or
narrative identity theory?

**Synergy Surplus**
How would one operationalize and measure the "extra value" that
emerges from a relationship beyond the sum of its members? What
econometric framework could capture this? Could it be quantified
through health outcomes, productivity, or some composite measure?

**Time-Inverted Identity**
What cryptographic primitives would best implement an identity
based on accumulated process rather than current coordinates?
Existing work on Soulbound Tokens (Buterin et al.) and Self-Sovereign
Identity provides starting points but does not yet integrate the
time-decay or process-as-signature concepts.

**Marking System Safeguards**
How would one design a legal-technical framework for proportional,
time-decaying, recoverable identity marks that does not collapse
into a Chinese-style social credit system? What is the minimum
viable due process?

**Trust as System Asset**
Can the "trust capital" of a system be measured? Can the relationship
between system trust and surveillance pressure be formalized? Is
there a tipping point beyond which trust collapses surveillance need?

## Where to Publish

The manifesto suggests these concepts deserve serious academic
treatment. Suitable venues:

- *Philosophy & Technology*
- *Big Data & Society*
- *Ethics and Information Technology*
- *Journal of Social Philosophy*
- Conferences: FAccT, AIES, 4S

If you write a paper based on these concepts, please cite this
repository and open an issue labeled `paper` with a link.
