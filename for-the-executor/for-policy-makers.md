# For Policy Makers

The manifesto sketches structural alternatives to the 1-person
basis of most current law and policy. The smallest legible policy
experiment is described below.

## A Pilot: Relational Unit Registration

Currently, most policies are designed around the individual taxpayer,
voter, or household. A relational unit registration would create
a new category, lighter than marriage but legally recognized.

**Definition**
Two or more adults voluntarily registering as a "relational unit,"
declaring that they share life context — meals, rhythms, mutual
support, conscious choice of each other's presence.

**Benefits**
Small but tangible:
- Tax deduction equivalent to the per-person standard deduction × 1.1
  (the 10% bonus is the "synergy surplus" recognized economically)
- Health insurance — members can be co-insured under one plan
- Housing — qualify for family-sized rental subsidies as a unit
- Medical decisions — members have visitation and decision rights
  for each other

**Limits**
- Annual renewal required (no permanent lock-in)
- Maximum 5 members per unit
- Cannot stack with marriage
- No tax penalty for *not* being in a unit

**Why a Pilot**
This is best tried at a small jurisdictional level first — a single
city, a single province — with a 3-year sunset clause and explicit
evaluation criteria (mental health metrics, fertility rates,
loneliness indicators).

## What This Does Not Solve

This policy alone cannot fix the pyramid society. It is one small
opening. Its main value is making *relational units* legible to
the legal system at all — which is currently impossible for any
arrangement that is neither marriage nor family of origin.
