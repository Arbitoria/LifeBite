# For Educators

The manifesto's deepest claim about education is that the current
system teaches surface signals (credentials, scores) when the
actual need is teaching **contextual sense** and **the gap between
stimulus and response**.

## A Possible Semester

A 16-week course called something like "Relational Intelligence" or
"Education That Teaches the Gap."

Weeks 1-4: **Attention**
- Meditation practice (any tradition)
- Reading: passages from contemplative traditions
- Exercise: notice the gap between impulse and action

Weeks 5-8: **Context**
- Nonviolent communication basics
- Reading another person's actual situation, not stereotype
- Exercise: paired listening without giving advice

Weeks 9-12: **LifeBite Sharing**
- Daily structured exchange in pairs
- Writing about what was shared without summarizing
- Exercise: notice what changes after sustained sharing

Weeks 13-16: **Cell Formation**
- 3-5 person cells form, with simple operating principles
- The cell decides one shared activity, one shared expense,
  one shared responsibility, for the remainder of the semester
- Reflection: what did the cell teach that solo study could not?

## Assessment Without Grading

The course concludes with a reflective written piece, but no
letter grade. The student keeps a portfolio of weekly entries
and presents to a small group.

## How to Start

You don't need permission to teach this. You can run it as:

- A free Saturday workshop in your community
- A reading group at your library
- An after-school program for older students
- A workplace learning circle
- A homeschool curriculum

If you do, please share what happens. Open an issue here labeled
`education-pilot`.
