# Risks and Safeguards

The marking system and related mechanisms in the manifesto are the
heaviest tools in the framework. The full risk catalogue (R1–R12)
is in [`glossary.md`](glossary.md) Section 6.

## Key Risks Summary

- **R1.** Marking system power abuse — risk of social credit system drift
- **R2.** Trust network homogenization — echo chamber risk
- **R3.** Power asymmetry within relational units
- **R4.** Weak identity for those with short histories (children, immigrants)
- **R5.** Loss of recovery/change rights
- **R6.** LifeBite verification failure
- **R7.** Coercion into relational unit system
- **R8.** AI as double-edged sword (replacement vs partner)
- **R9.** Early experiment failure breeding cynicism
- **R10.** Displacement of bubble industry workers
- **R11.** Public shame and guilt-by-association
- **R12.** Surveillance pressure from trust deficit

Each risk has a proposed safeguard. See [`glossary.md`](glossary.md)
Section 6 for full discussion.

## The Most Important Safeguard

**Trust as the system's primary asset.** Most other safeguards collapse
if the underlying system itself cannot be trusted by participants.
This is why every implementation must start small, with verifiable
behavior, before scaling.
