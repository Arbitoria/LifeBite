# After the Pyramid

## A Manifesto for the Next Social Architecture

*Squeezing the bubble, creating another one*

---

### Preface — Why This Document Exists

This document is a letter from one person to others.

The exterior of our society still looks fine. Companies run, trains move, shops open. Yet the interior of many people who live inside it is rotting at speed. Loneliness has become routine. Marriage and childbirth feel like burdens. Even meeting a friend is now perceived as a cost. Work has lost meaning. The more we are exposed to media, the more hollow we become.

None of this is a personal failure. **For five thousand years, humanity has been organized as a pyramid — and that pyramid has finally reached the limit of what it can hold.** The shape still stands, but the weight distribution inside has already inverted. The bubbles that once supported us can no longer hold the load.

This document sets out the diagnosis. Then it sketches the outline of a possible next social unit. It is not a master plan. It is a **seed**, organized in a form that can travel. Someone will read it. Someone will quote it. Someone will start a small experiment. That is enough.

The one who plants the seed and the one who harvests are, by nature, different people.

## Part 1. Diagnosis — Why We Are Rotting Inside an Intact Pyramid

### 1.1 Five Thousand Years of the Pyramid

The pyramid is not just a tomb. It was humanity's first organizational chart. Khufu's Great Pyramid at Giza was built around 4,500 years ago, and its form has shaped almost every social structure since — state, military, corporation, school, religion.

The essence of the pyramid is twofold. First, **the visualization of hierarchy**: a single apex and a wide base, carved into stone for eternity. This image has imprinted on humanity for five thousand years the unconscious belief that "hierarchy is natural and permanent." Second, **vertical delegation of weight**: every layer delegates its load to the layer immediately below, which in turn delegates further down. The system is not "the top pressing down on the bottom" but **"the bottom holding up the top — and only by doing so does the top exist."**

This form worked well for forty-five centuries. In agrarian societies, industrial societies, and the early stages of the information society. Then two conditions changed, and the cracks began.

### 1.2 The First Asymmetry — Inner Expansion vs Outer Stasis

The first crack came from **the expansion of the human interior.** Since the Enlightenment, humanity has slowly grown the self-recognition: "I am a sovereign equal in dignity." Religious reform, civic revolution, human rights declarations, feminism, the information revolution — each step has widened and deepened the individual interior.

But the external structure that has to contain this expanded interior is still the Bronze Age pyramid. **The interior is in the 21st century; the exterior is still in the 3rd millennium BCE.** This mismatch is the source of the unconscious suffocation so many people feel.

### 1.3 The Second Asymmetry — The Base Is Shrinking, Permanently

The pyramid is structurally a system that **requires the bottom to be ever wider** in order to function. One chairman requires thousands of new entrants behind him. One pensioner requires multiple contributors behind him. Yet for the first time in human history, the base is **permanently** narrowing.

South Korea's total fertility rate has fallen to 0.7. In simple terms: across three generations, the population becomes one-tenth its current size. The pyramid's exterior holds, but its interior has been an inverted triangle for some time now.

The system keeps standing only because of life support: national debt, imported labor, automation, inflated asset prices. When this life support stops, the pyramid will collapse from within. That moment is closer than most assume.

### 1.4 The Bubble Froth — What It Really Is

For the pyramid to keep working, it needs **devices that justify vertical movement**. Someone has to climb. And the act of climbing must be continually signaled as "normal and desirable." This signaling industry is what I call the bubble.

In Korea, anyone can name them:

**Real estate bubble** — Housing is essentially a tool for dwelling. But it has been twisted into a ladder upward. About 70% of price is not dwelling value but **class-signaling value.**

**Private education bubble** — Education is essentially the cultivation of persons. But it has been twisted into a ticket to push one's children into the upper layers of the pyramid. Korea spends nearly 7% of GDP on private education for this reason.

**Credentialism bubble** — Not capacity to do work, but certificates to pass pyramid gates.

**Wedding and funeral industry bubbles** — Sacred passages of two people uniting or one person departing, twisted into stages for displaying social position.

**Brand consumption bubble** — A 2 million won handbag sells not because it carries things, but because it signals "I can be in that place."

**Middle management bubble** — Roles that produce no real value, existing only to broker between upper and lower layers.

**Financial speculation bubble** — Capital that self-multiplies disconnected from real value.

What all these bubbles share: **they vanish the instant the pyramid's vertical structure dissolves.** Without the ladder, the real estate class signal is meaningless. The credential becomes useless. The luxury sign collapses. Middle management ceases to exist.

The bubble is not the byproduct of the pyramid. **The bubble is the essence of the pyramid.** As long as the pyramid stands, the bubble will keep growing. **To eliminate the bubble, the pyramid itself must change form.**

## Part 2. The Invention of Units — LifeBite and Another One

### 2.1 LifeBite — The Real Unit of Human Difference

Every attempt to represent humans in the AI era ultimately reduces us to 64-bit, 128-bit, tokens, embeddings. This is why AI can imitate humans intricately yet **never capture the real difference between two human beings.**

The unit that makes humans human exists in a different dimension. **LifeBite** — the kimchi stew you had for lunch yesterday, the time you forgot your umbrella in the rain, the song that suddenly broke your heart, the single sentence from someone that demolished you. These microscopic choices and experiences are the actual building blocks that make a human being unlike anyone else.

Eight billion people differ from each other not because of DNA, environment, or neural circuits. They differ because **eight billion configurations of LifeBites are being accumulated, differently, in every moment.** Identical twins raised in the same house become different people through micro-differences in LifeBite accumulation.

This concept is decisive because — it is perhaps the only definition that can answer "what is a human being?" in the AI era. **AI is made of 64-bit. Humans are made of LifeBite. These two will never be the same species.**

### 2.2 Sharing Equals Convergence

When two people share a LifeBite, two lives pass through the same point at the same time. Once shared, that LifeBite is permanently part of both people.

The dinners eaten together, the films watched together, the crises survived together — when these accumulate, the two people's LifeBite sets begin to substantially overlap. At that point the two people are not merely "becoming similar" but **converging into a single entity.**

The reason a family is truly a family is not blood. It is that for decades they ate at the same table, observed the same holidays, traversed the same sorrows. **The mass of shared LifeBite binds them into one.** The same principle explains why adopted children become full children, why old friends become like family.

"Family" was never the name of a blood relation. It was always the name of **LifeBite accumulation.** We mistook it for blood.

### 2.3 Another One — A New Social Unit

Every social system to date has organized humans as **"one."** One individual, one citizen, one worker, one consumer. Every institution operates on this "one." This is how we arrived at the radical individualism of today.

But human potential is realized when we make **"another one."** Not the I and the you, but a third one that lives in the space between two people.

Hannah Arendt called this **"the space in-between."** Martin Buber called it **"I-Thou."** Korean has a more precise word — **정 (jeong)**: the thing that exists between two people, belongs to neither, yet transforms both more deeply than anything else.

The first society to elevate this **jeong** to the basic unit of social operation — that is the starting point of what comes next.

### 2.4 Synergy Surplus — Not Sum, But New Value

All current cooperation, partnership, and marriage models operate as **"1 + 1 = 2."** Sum of two labors, sum of two assets. Mere arithmetic.

The new model is **"1 + 1 = 2.3."** Where does the 0.3 come from? From **synergy surplus** — the value generated by the relationship itself, by two people sharing LifeBite.

This surplus is a new form of value society must recognize and reward. Because at the social scale, the more deep relational units exist, the lower the cost of mental healthcare, suicide, declining birthrates, crime, elderly care, and the loneliness economy. **A portion of these savings should be returned as a bonus to relational units — redirecting what society now spends on processing loneliness into preventing it.** This is the economic foundation of the new society.

## Part 3. Three Pillars — Education, Network, Identity

The bubble is not floating in the air. It is **produced every day in three specific factories.** Education teaches surface signals. Networks lull us with false stimulation. Identity confines us to externally-defined selves. **All three must be squeezed at once, or the bubble will regrow.**

### 3.1 Education — From "For One" to "For Context"

Every unit of education to date has been **the one.** Tests taken alone, scores received alone, ranks assigned alone. **A person raised as the perpetual object of solitary evaluation becomes a person unable to form deep relationships with others.**

The new education teaches different things. **What context is** — the invisible weave of meaning that flows between persons, and how to perceive it. **Why context matters** — the existence of value that cannot be held alone, that emerges only between two. **How to choose well** — the capacity to judge, from essentials, with whom to walk. **How to be chosen** — not by appearance or credential, but by training the depth of inner self.

The most decisive part is the last. **Begin with inner cultivation, not external appearance.** Almost everything current education teaches is ultimately surface signal — credentials, scores, looks, specs. All bubble. Inner cultivation moves in the opposite direction: **the capacity to remain aligned with oneself when unseen**, **the capacity to bear solitude**, **the capacity to truly receive another's LifeBite.**

This kind of education has been attempted at the margins by Pestalozzi, Steiner, Montessori, Krishnamurti's schools. Now is the time to bring it into the center.

### 3.2 Network — From "Exposure Algorithm" to "Trust Algorithm"

The deepest subjugation humanity now faces: **"I no longer see through my own algorithm; I am shown by the web's algorithm."** Slavery bound the body. Feudalism bound the land. Capitalism bound labor. But **algorithmic capitalism binds consciousness itself.** The inability to decide what enters one's own eyes is the most fundamental dispossession of sovereignty.

**A world already paid for, before being seen** — this is the key that breaks the chain.

The current model: "watch for free, pay in advertising" — the advertiser is the real customer, the user is the product. Algorithms optimize for maximum attention capture. Clickbait, outrage, stimulation become the highest values. Bubble explodes.

The new model: "prepay in trust; the trust shows what it will show" — the user becomes the real customer. Algorithms optimize for trust-network coherence. Outrage loses trust and dies. Bubble exits on its own.

The deepest foundation of this model is **the asymmetry of time.** The bubble is made of stimulation and is quickly spent. Trust accumulates slowly and lasts long. **The moment time is introduced, bubble and essence separate automatically.**

There is a risk: trust networks can homogenize into echo chambers. Two safeguards: **intentional cross-trust networks** (each person belongs to multiple trust networks simultaneously) and **intentional discomfort injection** (regular, agreed-upon exposure to perspectives outside one's trust network).

### 3.3 Identity — From "Defensive Blocking" to "Sovereign Permitting"

Current system essence: **"Default is exposure; blocking is exception."** If your phone number is known, anyone can call. Spam must be blocked one by one. Email, social media, even strangers on the street operate on this principle.

New system essence: **"Default is blocking; permission is exception (via a ticket I issue)."** Your phone number can be public, but without a QR ticket you've issued, no signal reaches you. Email, the same — un-ticketed mail is returned to sender immediately. All messages, calls, requests reach you only from pre-authorized IDs. Tickets can be permanent, single-use, time-limited, or context-limited.

The depth of change: **the restoration of the freedom not to be attacked.** We currently live under constant non-consensual information intrusion — spam, ads, alerts, unknown calls, street billboards, elevator screens, restaurant TVs. The accumulation of these intrusions occupies our unconscious.

**The right to hold a shield in front of myself.** This is the most basic of human rights, yet was the first to be stripped in the digital era.

### 3.4 The Three Pillars Form a Single Loop

Current vicious cycle:
Education teaches surface signals → Spec bubble proliferates → Entry into network with these specs → Algorithm pulls us in with stimulation → Identity forms from exposure → Externally-defined self → That self chases more surface signals → Loop continues.

New virtuous cycle:
Education teaches contextual sense → Capacity for good choice → Trust network formed with this capacity → Only valuable information and relationships enter → Identity forms from those → Self I have chosen → That self perceives better contexts → Loop deepens.

The single principle running through all three pillars: **Sovereign Filter.** The right to decide what enters my consciousness. In education: the capacity to judge what is valuable. In network: the right to decide what to see. In identity: the right to permit who reaches me.

This is the real substance of "returning sovereignty." Not an abstract right, but **three concrete filters returned to the hand of the individual.**

## Part 4. Time Inversion — When Process Outweighs Result

### 4.1 Putting the Past in Front

Every society to date has defined identity by **current coordinates.** National ID number, credit score, job title, address, photograph — all point to who you are right now. The past has always been an appendage that trails behind.

The new system inverts this entirely. **The body of identity becomes "who I have been until now" (process), and "who I am now" (present) becomes the living, private flow behind that body.**

This is not a simple reordering. It is **reversing the direction of time within identity itself.** The past no longer trails into the future; **the past becomes the surface of identity, and the present hides behind it.**

### 4.2 An Elegant Solution to GDPR

GDPR's essential fear: "If someone knows my current coordinates, they can reach me." If they know the address, they come. If they know the phone, they call. If they know the health data, they discriminate.

In the new system, **"my current coordinates" are no longer part of identity at all.** Identity is the pattern formed by accumulated LifeBite — **the path signature of how I came to be here.** Even if this signature is exposed, there is no way to reach the current me physically. The signature is a past pattern, not a key linked to body, address, or account.

Encryption assumes there is something to protect, then locks the key. Keys eventually break. The new approach **removes the protected object from identity altogether.** The current me is an anonymous flow; identity is the past pattern. A thief who steals the pattern cannot find the current me. **Nothing to hide means nothing to steal.**

### 4.3 Identity Theft Becomes Structurally Impossible

If identity is a pattern of decades of LifeBite accumulation, theft itself becomes nearly impossible. Creating that pattern requires **time actually lived.** Stealing the pattern doesn't allow continuation — if the person ate kimchi stew yesterday but the thief lives a different pattern today, coherence breaks. AI can generate fake patterns, but **synchronizing in real-time with another person's trust network is impossible.**

This applies the principle of blockchain to human identity. **The continuity of time itself becomes the security of identity.** And identity grows stronger over time. Where all current identity systems become more exposed and weaker as time passes, this system becomes thicker and more secure.

### 4.4 Surface Signals Are Neutralized

A line of educational pedigree no longer proves anything. Neither does a luxury bag. Neither does a Gangnam address. **Thirty years of LifeBite accumulation becomes the real identity.** All signals that can be purchased in a short time lose value. The foundation of the bubble industry itself disappears.

"Process matters more than result" — for the first time, this value is **structurally enforced at the system level.** Not as moral exhortation but as structural fact.

## Part 5. The Ethical Frame — Interior and Exterior

### 5.1 Truth and Falsity — Both Become LifeBite

Whatever I do — true or false — **all of it makes bits.** Lies become LifeBite. Avoidance becomes LifeBite. Violence becomes LifeBite.

Every ethical system to date has tried to consider only "good actions" as part of the self. Bad acts have been quarantined as "not really me," "a mistake," "circumstances beyond control." The new system refuses this separation. **All bits are mine.**

This appears ruthless but is actually the deepest form of respect for the human being. To truly acknowledge the responsibility of choice is to acknowledge the person as free.

### 5.2 The New Boundary — Interior Is Absolutely Private, Exterior Is Absolutely Public

Here lies the answer to a problem liberalism failed to resolve for three hundred years.

**Thought is absolutely private. Action is absolutely public.**

The thought of killing — 100% the person's own, no one else's domain.
The act of killing — 100% public, must be inscribed in LifeBite.

The elegance of this boundary: no one can censor another's mind, and no one can escape their own action. **The interior is completely free; the exterior is completely accountable.**

### 5.3 The Real Task of Education — Teaching the Gap

Between thought and action, there is always a **gap.** The microscopic time between an impulse and its translation into action. For most people, this gap is short or nearly nonexistent. Yet **this gap can be consciously expanded through training.**

What meditation traditions have taught for 2,500 years, what martial arts at its core teaches, the cultivation of self in Eastern traditions, Stoic self-mastery, ascetic ataraxia, Korean *seonbi* ethos, Japanese *bushido*, Indian yoga — all were training in widening this gap.

Modern education does not teach this. It teaches test scores only. The result is a generation rich in knowledge but ever-weakening in self-mastery.

The new system reverses the priority of education. **"What to know" matters less than "what to remain between."**

### 5.4 The Marking System — The Heaviest Tool

If action is identity, then the consequences of action toward others must also be **explicitly inscribed in that person's identity.** This is the **marking system.** Through legally-recognized procedure, the authority to insert a mark into someone's LifeBite.

This system institutionalizes the Eastern tradition of **shin-dok (慎獨)** — to remain careful even when alone. The sense that "even when no one is watching, the LifeBite is watching, and that is who I become."

But this is the heaviest tool in 5,000 years of human history. Designed well, it is a leap in human ethics. Designed badly, it becomes a new dystopia. Several safeguards must be carried alongside it.

**Who holds the authority to mark** — Misdesigned, this becomes the Chinese social credit system. **Due process** — Inscribing a permanent mark on identity may be heavier than the death penalty. Trial + appeal + multi-verification are minimum requirements. **Prohibition of anonymous mob marking** — This must never become a tool for cancel culture. **Grading and proportionality of marks** — Petty rudeness and serious crime must not carry the same weight. **Protection against power asymmetry** — The weak must be able to mark the powerful; the powerful must not be able to unilaterally mark the weak. **Punishment of false marking** — False marking must be inscribed even more heavily in the false-marker's LifeBite.

### 5.5 The Dynamics of Time — Not Permanent Stigma but a Recoverable System

Marks are not eternal. Their weight must decay over time.

The decisive feature of this system — **today's action carries more weight in tomorrow's identity than past marks do.** A wrong from ten years ago gradually loses weight; today's choice becomes the core of the renewed identity. The fact that identity is a living flow is implemented through this time decay.

Even more important — **a mark for which sufficient consequence has been paid must be re-marked.** An "unresolved" mark transitions to a "resolved" mark. The mark does not disappear, but its meaning changes. Two faces of truth are inscribed together: "this person did this, and completed their responsibility for it."

Designed this way, the system becomes **a tool of recovery, not of punishment.** The recognition of the most basic human dignity — that a person can change.

### 5.6 Private Burden and Value Redirection — Structural Flow, Not Public Shame

Another critical design principle of the marking system — **the disadvantage of the mark falls on the person, but others do not know it is them.**

All previous forms of social punishment — the scarlet letter, public execution, cancel culture — depended on **public shame.** Suppressing behavior by making everyone know. This was effective but merciless, and often unjust.

The new system goes a different way. The mark affects **structural value flow.** For example: a person who has harmed another can continue working, but **a portion of the value of that work flows to the victim, or to a societal recovery fund.** The work itself is not stopped; the compensation flows differently.

This way:
- The marked person can live an ordinary life (not excluded)
- The victim receives real recovery
- Society reduces the cost of housing/sustaining the offender
- The mark works without public shaming
- The offender's family and children do not suffer through guilt-by-association

This is **a system of justice that is lighter than the death penalty yet more precise.** The weight of the mark is borne by the person, but the weight becomes a recoverable structural cost rather than social character assassination.

### 5.7 The Real Problem Is the Absence of Trust

Here the deepest insight emerges.

The reason our current society pushes everything into public disclosure — name, address, education, income, criminal record, credit score, medical record — is that **there is no trust in the system.** When the system is inaccurate, people must verify information themselves. So pressure builds for everything to be exposed.

If the system could truly be trusted — if marks were accurate, if they decayed over time, if they were processed as private burden, if they were applied fairly — **people would not need to know everything.** The belief that the system handles it removes the pressure for individual disclosure.

The core insight: **"It is not a question of how much one receives. It is a question of an inaccurate system. The problem exists because there is no trust."**

This is the root cause of all surveillance capitalism, all doxxing, all public executions. **The absence of trust.** So the most important asset of the new system is not technology, not algorithms, not law. **It is the degree to which the system itself can be trusted.**

This is not built at once. Small trust accumulates, is verified, and larger trust accumulates on top. So we begin in small cells, small tools, small experiments — because these become **the first units of trust.**

### 5.8 The Balance of Yin and Yang

The marking system is not only punitive. **Good actions must also be markable by another.** The one who received help marks the one who gave it. The mentee marks the mentor. Colleague marks colleague.

Designed this way, the system is not a punishment system. It becomes **a mirror of truth.** All meaningful actions a person performs — good and bad — remain as traces within identity.

In essence, this is the social-structural implementation of the **karma** concept of Buddhism. Every act leaves a result, and the accumulation of those results is the self.

## Part 6. The Squeezing Hand and the Place to Be Squeezed

### 6.1 The Sponge Principle

The bubble pretends to have no weight, but it takes up space. Only when that space is emptied does the real enter. And the decisive point — **the sponge does not forget its shape.** We do not need to invent a new form. **Squeeze the bubble, and the original form reveals itself.**

This is the core working principle. **Not "what to build anew" but "what to squeeze out."** Subtraction, not addition. Purification, not construction.

### 6.2 But — The Bubble Itself Is Not Evil

There is one thing we must be honest about. **The bubble itself is not evil.**

The bubble is precisely useful in its proper context — for washing away grease, for cleaning. Advertising creates value where genuine information is needed. Brand signals carry meaning in transactions that require quick identification. Credentials work when fast verification of specific capability is needed. Middle management creates value where actual coordination work exists.

**The problem is not the bubble's existence but its location.** When the bubble is inside the sponge — in the wrong context — everything goes wrong. The sponge's task is absorption and cleaning, but if it is full of bubble, it cannot do its work.

So the real name of this work is not "bubble eradication" but **"contextual restoration."** Letting each thing return to its proper place. The Eastern expression: **各得其所 (gak-deuk-gi-so)** — everything finding its place.

Seen this way, the weight of the work changes. We are not trying to eradicate anyone. We are trying to **return what has been misplaced to its proper place.**

- Advertising as an information channel (not a tool that occupies consciousness)
- Brand as identification signal (not class marker)
- Credentials as certification of specific capability (not life-passes)
- Middle management as actual coordination work (not self-justifying industry)
- Real estate as a dwelling tool (not a social ladder)
- Media as a channel for transmitting trust (not stimulation addiction industry)

When each thing is in its place, it is no longer bubble. **It is the place that makes the bubble, and the place that releases it.**

### 6.3 First Squeezing — In Three Places

You cannot squeeze the whole society at once. You must first grasp the nearest bubble in your own hand.

**Squeeze the language** — Condense scattered insights into a form that survives without further conversation. Without this, nothing else is possible.

**Squeeze the vision** — Condense the same thought into a single diagram. A tool that travels faster than words.

**Squeeze the relationship** — Design a first cell of 3-5 people, concretely. Stronger evidence than any manifesto or image — **a small sample that actually works.**

### 6.4 Starting From a Small Cell

A 3-5 person intentional cell can be tried immediately.

Working principles:
- **Shared rituals** — daily brief LifeBite exchange (what did you eat, what was hard, what are you looking forward to)
- **Shared resources** — pooling some expenses (meals, books, subscriptions — start small)
- **Shared responsibility** — when one falls ill, others take partial responsibility
- **Re-contract cycle** — every six months, decide together whether to maintain, expand, or dissolve the cell
- **Appearance as one unit externally** — participating in events as the cell, making decisions as the cell

This is not marriage. Not a roommate arrangement. It is **"Context Family"** — a new form. 

When 100 such cells exist, when 1,000 exist, the existence itself becomes **proof of a new social mode.** A hundred times more persuasive than grand discourse.

### 6.5 Squeezing Bubble in Your Own Daily Life

Even while living inside the pyramid system, you can begin at the individual level to refuse bubble:

- Refuse consumption that functions as class signal
- Stop activities aimed only at vertical movement
- Increase direct relationships that bypass intermediary layers
- Invest more time in deep LifeBite exchange

When this is begun consciously, you will be surprised by how much time and money were being drained. **That recovered resource becomes the fuel for the next stage.**

## Part 7. To the Executor

If you are reading this and find yourself in a position of power, or with the capacity to build tools, or able to gather a small community — please consider the following.

This document is not a grand blueprint. A 5,000-year-old system cannot be changed by a single policy or a single technology. **But within this thinking are several pathways that can begin as small seeds.**

**If you are a tool designer** — Build a small open-source tool that implements one thread of the Sovereign Filter. Examples: phone number ticket system, trust-based content curation, relational-unit financial app, cell-based decision tool. If even one such tool exists well-made and open, it becomes the foundation for the next layer of thinking.

**If you work in education** — A semester-length pilot program for "education that teaches the gap." Integrating meditation, nonviolent communication, relational writing, contextual awareness training into a small learning unit. Start with one school, one class.

**If you design communities** — A 5-10 person "Context Family" first experiment, for one year. Apply the cell operating principles. Record the process so the next cell can reference it.

**If you make policy** — A pilot of **relational-unit policy** rather than individual-unit policy. A small structure where registering as two or more grants certain tax, housing, and healthcare benefits. Not marriage, but something lighter and freer.

**If you invest** — Capital flow into **connection industries**, not bubble industries. Trust-based content platforms, tools that generate relational value, cell-level collaboration infrastructure.

**If you research** — Academic refinement of the concepts here: LifeBite, synergy surplus, time-inverted identity. These ideas are at vocabulary level; they have not yet reached theory level.

**Anyone** — Read this document with one other person, and try one thing together. The first conscious LifeBite exchange between two people.

You do not need to attempt something enormous. **The space the bubble leaves fills itself with good things.** One first hand squeezing is enough.
